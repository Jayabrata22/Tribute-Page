Rabindranath Tagore

1861-1941

A Bengali polymath

Rabindranath Tagore ( 7 May 1861 , 7 August 1941) was a Bengali polymathâ€”poet, writer, playwright, composer, philosopher, social reformer and painter. He reshaped Bengali literature and music as well as Indian art with Contextual Modernism in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. Author of the "profoundly sensitive, fresh and beautiful" poetry of Gitanjali,he became in 1913 the first non-European and the first lyricist to win the Nobel Prize in Literature. Tagore's poetic songs were viewed as spiritual and mercurial; however, his "elegant prose and magical poetry" remain largely unknown outside Bengal. He was a fellow of the Royal Asiatic Society. Referred to as "the Bard of Bengal", Tagore was known by sobriquets: Gurudev, Kobiguru, Biswakobi.

Following list is a Time Line Of Rabindranath Tagor's Life:-

  1. 7 May 1861 Born in Calcutta, India
  2. 1868 Wrote his first poem when he was just 7 years
  3. 1878 Tagore's first book of poetries appeared
  4. 1878 Went to England for higher studies
  5. 1879 Came back to India
  6. 1883 Got married to Mrinalini Devi
  7. 1890 Manasi, a collection of Tagore's poems were published
  8. 1890 Settled at Shilaidaha in Kushthia (now in Bangladesh)
  9. 1901 Founded school at Shanti Niketan
  10. 1905 Bengal was partitioned, Rabindranath was gripped by fever of Nationalism
  11. 1907 Rabindranath's wife, father, daughter Renuka and son Samindra died
  12. 1910 He got married his son to a young widow Pratima Devi. He was a staunch supporter of widow remarriage
  13. 1910 Wrote Gitanjali in Bengali
  14. 1911 Wrote Jana Gana Mana which later became our National Anthem
  15. 1912 Gitanjali was published in English
  16. 1913 Awarded Nobel prize in literature
  17. 1915 Knighted by the British king George
  18. 1919 Renounced his knighthood following Jallianwala massacre incident
  19. 1924 Tagore opened Vishva Bharati University at Shanti Niketan
  20. 1929 Tagore began painting
  21. 1939 Tagore asked Gandhiji to lift the ban on Subhash Chandra Bose and have his cooperation cordially invited in the "supreme interest of national unity"
  22. 1941 Rabindranath Tagore died in Calcutta, India
